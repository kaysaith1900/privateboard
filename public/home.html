How a Room Works

Three movements: convene · sharpen · adjourn.

No calendars, no quorum, no warm-up. The chair runs the room. You hold the floor.

01 / convene

Bring a question. Invite 2–4 directors.

Pose what's on your mind — a strategy, a decision, a theory. Pick the directors whose lenses you need most. Mix opposing styles for sharper rooms.

02 / sharpen

Set the tone. Hold the floor.

Dial the temperature from gentle brainstorm to systematic critique. Push back, redirect, or just listen as the directors take turns testing your thinking.

03 / adjourn

Walk away with a multi-lens analysis.

The room distills into a sharp, multi-perspective analysis: one-sentence thesis up top, each director's strongest argument attributed under it, the disagreements that didn't resolve kept intact. Forward it, file it, or use it to convene the next room.

Modes

Same room. Different temperature.

Four rooms, one cast. Brainstorm to open the surface area, debate to test the obvious answer, critique to break what's already committed, research to chase what you don't yet know. The chair enforces the rhythm so the same six directors play four different games.

What You Take Away

A multi-lens analysis. Not a transcript.

Every room ends with a structured, multi-perspective analysis on disk. The chair distills the discussion into a single load-bearing thesis, each director's strongest argument attributed under it, and the disagreements that didn't resolve — preserved verbatim, never smoothed into false consensus.

Under the hood

A harness, not a wrapper.

Most "AI products" are a single model in a chat box. PrivateBoard is the engine that binds intelligence to identity — multi-model routing, durable memory, real skills. Each director runs on the model you assign, remembers what mattered, and reaches for tools when the question demands it.

multi-model

Any model. Any director.

Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, xAI, DeepSeek — wire any combination per agent. Pin opus to the skeptic, gpt-5 to the strategist, haiku to the chair. Bring your own keys; pay only the inference you actually use.

memory

They remember you.

Per-director, per-room, durable across sessions. Pin facts you want every future room to see; let routine context decay. The chair carries what mattered into the final analysis.

skills

Tools when the question demands.

Web search when the answer needs fresh data. URL fetch when you share a link. Per-agent skill grids — opt in only what each director should reach for. New skills shipping each release.

Why People Convene

The questions that benefit most.

01

That startup thesis. That product bet. That contrarian view you're about to publish. Your board will find the structural flaws before the market does.
